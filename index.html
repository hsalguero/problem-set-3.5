Basics

Football is defined as a form of team game played in North America with an oval ball on a field marked out as a gridiron. It is popular mostly in the United States and has millions of fans around the country. The most popular football league in the United States is the National Football League (NFL). College Football is also popular among football fans and has gained a large following as well.

Each football team has three different groups of players that come on the field at different times. Whenever possession is changed, the players on the field switch with whichever group is needed on the field at that time. The three groups are:

  1. Offense - Is in charge of moving the football down the field
  2. Defense - Is in charge of stoppiing the offense from moving the ball down the field
  3. Special Teams - Comes on the field during a kick or punt to the other team

Sizing

The size of a football varies depending on the level you play on, whether it be a national, college, high school, or youth league. As you move up, you will play with slightly larger footballs that can take a little getting used to.
